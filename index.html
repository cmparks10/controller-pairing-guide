Xbox Series X Controller Guide

×

Powering On

  1. Press and hold the Xbox button (the large circular button with the Xbox logo) for 2-3 seconds
  2. The Xbox button will start flashing, indicating the controller is turning on
  3. Once the button stops flashing and stays lit, the controller is powered on

Pairing to Xbox Series X/S

  1. Turn on your Xbox Series X or Series S console
  2. Press and hold the Pair button on your Xbox console (located on the front panel)
  3. While the console is searching, press and hold the Pair button on your controller (located on the top edge, next to the USB-C port)
  4. Hold both buttons until the Xbox button on the controller starts flashing rapidly
  5. When the Xbox button stops flashing and stays lit, the controller is paired

Pairing to Windows PC

  1. Make sure your PC has Bluetooth capability
  2. Open Windows Settings → Devices → Bluetooth & other devices
  3. Click "Add Bluetooth or other device" → "Bluetooth"
  4. Press and hold the Pair button on your controller until the Xbox button flashes rapidly
  5. Select "Xbox Wireless Controller" from the list of available devices
  6. Click "Done" when the pairing is complete

Pairing to Steam Deck

  1. On your Steam Deck, go to Settings → Bluetooth
  2. Make sure Bluetooth is turned ON
  3. Press and hold the Pair button on your Xbox controller until the Xbox button flashes rapidly
  4. On the Steam Deck, tap "Add Device"
  5. Select "Xbox Wireless Controller" from the list of available devices
  6. Tap "Pair" to complete the connection
  7. The Xbox button should stop flashing and stay lit when successfully paired
Note: The Xbox Series X controller works natively with Steam Deck. You may need to update your Steam Deck's firmware for optimal compatibility.

Pairing to Android Device

  1. Open your Android device's Settings → Connected devices → Pair new device
  2. Press and hold the Pair button on your controller until the Xbox button flashes rapidly
  3. Select "Xbox Wireless Controller" from the available devices
  4. Tap "Pair" to complete the connection

Troubleshooting

  • Controller won't turn on: Try replacing the batteries or charging via USB-C cable
  • Won't pair: Make sure you're within 6-8 feet of the console/device
  • Connection drops: Check for interference from other wireless devices
  • Button not responding: Try resetting the controller by holding the Xbox button for 10 seconds
  • Steam Deck issues: Try restarting the Steam Deck or updating its firmware

8BitDo SN30 Pro Guide

×

Powering On

  1. Press and hold the Start button for 2-3 seconds
  2. The LED indicator will light up, showing the current connection mode
  3. Different LED patterns indicate different connection modes:
    • Solid green: X-Input mode (Windows)
    • Solid blue: D-Input mode (Android, macOS)
    • Solid yellow: Switch mode
    • Solid red: macOS mode

Pairing to Nintendo Switch

  1. On your Switch, go to System Settings → Controllers and Sensors → Pro Controller Wired Communication
  2. Turn this setting OFF
  3. Go to Change Grip/Order screen
  4. Press and hold Y + Start on your SN30 Pro for 3 seconds
  5. The LED should turn yellow, indicating Switch mode
  6. Your controller should appear on the Switch's pairing screen
  7. Select it to complete pairing

Pairing to Windows PC

  1. Press and hold X + Start for 3 seconds to enter X-Input mode
  2. The LED should turn green
  3. Open Windows Settings → Devices → Bluetooth & other devices
  4. Click "Add Bluetooth or other device" → "Bluetooth"
  5. Select "8BitDo SN30 Pro" from the available devices
  6. Click "Done" when pairing is complete

Pairing to Steam Deck

  1. On your Steam Deck, go to Settings → Bluetooth
  2. Make sure Bluetooth is turned ON
  3. Press and hold X + Start for 3 seconds to enter X-Input mode (LED should turn green)
  4. On the Steam Deck, tap "Add Device"
  5. Select "8BitDo SN30 Pro" from the list of available devices
  6. Tap "Pair" to complete the connection
  7. The LED should remain solid green when successfully paired
Note: Use X-Input mode (green LED) for best compatibility with Steam Deck. The controller will be recognized as a standard gamepad.

Pairing to Android Device

  1. Press and hold A + Start for 3 seconds to enter D-Input mode
  2. The LED should turn blue
  3. Open your Android device's Settings → Connected devices → Pair new device
  4. Select "8BitDo SN30 Pro" from the available devices
  5. Tap "Pair" to complete the connection

Pairing to macOS

  1. Press and hold B + Start for 3 seconds to enter macOS mode
  2. The LED should turn red
  3. Open System Preferences → Bluetooth
  4. Click the "+" button to add a new device
  5. Select "8BitDo SN30 Pro" from the list
  6. Click "Pair" to complete the connection

Troubleshooting

  • Controller won't turn on: Check if the battery is charged or replace batteries
  • Wrong mode: Use the correct button combination for your device type
  • Won't pair: Make sure the device's Bluetooth is enabled and in pairing mode
  • Connection issues: Try resetting the controller by holding Start for 10 seconds
  • Switch pairing issues: Ensure Pro Controller Wired Communication is OFF
  • Steam Deck issues: Make sure you're in X-Input mode (green LED) for best compatibility

Sony DualSense Guide

×

Powering On

  1. Press and hold the PS button (the large circular button with the PlayStation logo) for 2-3 seconds
  2. The light bar around the touchpad will illuminate, indicating the controller is turning on
  3. Once the light bar stops flashing and stays lit, the controller is powered on
  4. The light bar color indicates the player number (blue for player 1, red for player 2, etc.)

Pairing to PlayStation 5

  1. Turn on your PlayStation 5 console
  2. Connect the DualSense controller to the PS5 using the included USB-C cable
  3. Press the PS button on the controller
  4. The controller will automatically pair with the console
  5. Once paired, you can disconnect the USB cable and use the controller wirelessly
  6. For additional controllers, repeat the process with each controller

Pairing to Windows PC

  1. Make sure your PC has Bluetooth capability
  2. Open Windows Settings → Devices → Bluetooth & other devices
  3. Click "Add Bluetooth or other device" → "Bluetooth"
  4. Press and hold the PS button and Create button (small button above the left analog stick) simultaneously for 3 seconds
  5. The light bar will start flashing rapidly, indicating pairing mode
  6. Select "Wireless Controller" from the list of available devices
  7. Click "Done" when the pairing is complete
Note: For best compatibility on Windows, you may need to install DS4Windows or Steam for proper button mapping and functionality.

Pairing to Steam Deck

  1. On your Steam Deck, go to Settings → Bluetooth
  2. Make sure Bluetooth is turned ON
  3. Press and hold the PS button and Create button simultaneously for 3 seconds
  4. The light bar will start flashing rapidly
  5. On the Steam Deck, tap "Add Device"
  6. Select "Wireless Controller" from the list of available devices
  7. Tap "Pair" to complete the connection
  8. The light bar should stop flashing and stay lit when successfully paired
Note: The DualSense works well with Steam Deck, though some advanced features like haptic feedback may not be fully supported. Steam will automatically configure the controller mapping.

Pairing to Android Device

  1. Open your Android device's Settings → Connected devices → Pair new device
  2. Press and hold the PS button and Create button simultaneously for 3 seconds
  3. The light bar will start flashing rapidly
  4. Select "Wireless Controller" from the available devices
  5. Tap "Pair" to complete the connection
Note: Android support may vary by device and Android version. Some games may require additional controller mapping apps.

Pairing to macOS

  1. Open System Preferences → Bluetooth
  2. Make sure Bluetooth is turned ON
  3. Press and hold the PS button and Create button simultaneously for 3 seconds
  4. The light bar will start flashing rapidly
  5. Click the "+" button to add a new device
  6. Select "Wireless Controller" from the list
  7. Click "Pair" to complete the connection
Note: macOS support is limited. The controller may work with some games, but advanced features like haptic feedback and adaptive triggers are not supported.

Troubleshooting

  • Controller won't turn on: Try charging via USB-C cable for at least 30 minutes
  • Won't pair: Make sure you're within 6-8 feet of the console/device and using the correct button combination
  • Connection drops: Check for interference from other wireless devices or try moving closer to the console
  • Button not responding: Try resetting the controller by inserting a paperclip into the reset hole on the back
  • PC compatibility issues: Install DS4Windows or use Steam for better compatibility
  • Steam Deck issues: Try restarting the Steam Deck or updating its firmware
  • Light bar not working: Check if the controller is properly charged and try resetting it
